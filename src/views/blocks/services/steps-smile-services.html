Treatment Steps

The procedure typically takes 1-2 hours, and you may need a follow-up visit for the permanent restoration.

Consultation and Diagnosis

We assess your tooth, take X-rays, and determine the extent of the damage.

Numbing and Isolation

Local anesthesia ensures comfort, and a dental dam keeps the tooth dry during treatment.

Accessing and Cleaning the Tooth

A small opening is made to remove infected tissue and clean the root canals.

Sealing the Canals

The canals are sealed with a biocompatible material to prevent reinfection.

Restoration

A filling or crown is placed to restore the tooth's function and appearance.
